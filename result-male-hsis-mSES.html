Your Sexual Temperament Summary

Profile: High Inhibition (SIS) + Medium Excitation (SES)

Hey friend, your sexual temperament shows a mix of potential and protection. You’re capable of arousal — but only when the conditions are right. Your mind wants it, but your nervous system needs to trust it.

💡 What This Looks Like in Real Life

You may feel desire in a slow, steady way — a kiss, a deep conversation, the right music — it starts to build. But then something changes: the lights feel too bright, your partner seems distracted, or a sudden doubt enters your mind... and it’s gone. Your body hits the brakes.

It can feel frustrating. One moment, you’re tuned in. The next, you’re disconnected — and wondering what happened. But this isn’t dysfunction. It’s your system doing its job: staying emotionally safe, not just physically ready.

🧠 For Men Like You

This profile doesn’t mean you’re “low drive” or broken. It means you need **emotional congruence**. You need your mind, body, and environment to all say yes — not just one of them. And when they do? You can show up fully.

But here’s the catch: society rarely gives men permission to be this way. You’re expected to perform, not reflect. To push through, not check in. But your nervous system doesn’t lie — and it deserves respect.

✅ Supportive Tools for Your Wiring

  • 💬 Talk about pressure: stress or fear of letting someone down might be activating your SIS. The more openly you speak, the less control it has.
  • 🕯️ Curate intentional intimacy: set the mood, slow things down, stay grounded in the moment — your SES needs a soft runway.
  • 🪞 Get curious about your brakes: Is it fear? Shame? Disconnection? Name it, and you reclaim your power over it.
  • 📖 Learn your arousal style: fantasize, write, read, explore ideas that turn you on mentally — arousal often begins in your mind.
  • 🧠 Drop the “shoulds”: you don’t need to want it all the time. You need to want it when it’s real, safe, and mutual. That’s strength.
🔍 What do SIS and SES actually mean?

SIS (Sexual Inhibition System) is your brake — it responds quickly to emotional stress, shame, fear, or pressure. High SIS means you’re especially sensitive to these signals, and they can easily override arousal.

SES (Sexual Excitation System) is your gas pedal — it responds to intimacy, eroticism, and sensual context. A medium SES means your desire builds gradually, not instantly, and needs emotional support to stay active.

Put simply: You’re someone who thrives on trust, intention, and connection — not just stimulation.
