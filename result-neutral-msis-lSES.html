Your Sexual Temperament Summary

Profile: Medium Inhibition (SIS) + Low Excitation (SES)

Hey friend, your sexual system is thoughtful and cautious. You don’t get easily overwhelmed by fear or shame — but your arousal still tends to be quiet, slow to rise, and often depends on emotional or energetic alignment more than anything physical.

💡 What This Looks Like in Real Life

You might go through long stretches where sex or desire just doesn’t feel present. You may enjoy intimacy when it happens — but you’re rarely driven by urgency. And if the environment feels disconnected, rushed, or even slightly off… your interest may simply fade out without warning.

This can feel confusing — like you’re on the edge of wanting something, but not enough to chase it. That’s not dysfunction. That’s your system doing what it does best: asking for deeper signals before stepping into intimacy.

🌈 For Non-Binary, Fluid, and Unscripted Identities

You may have never seen yourself reflected in mainstream narratives about desire. Your experience might not be loud or linear. It may live in emotional resonance, in non-sexual forms of closeness, or in subtle moments that others might overlook.

You deserve to define intimacy on your own terms — not based on what others expect from your body, your identity, or your past. Desire doesn’t have to be constant to be valid.

✅ Supportive Practices for Your Emotional Tempo

  • 🧠 Pay attention to your "almost" moments: what nearly got you interested? What pulled you back? These clues matter.
  • 🕯️ Lean into calm connection: intimacy for you often begins with eye contact, softness, and emotional attunement — not stimulation.
  • 📖 Journal after intimacy: when it worked (or didn’t), write it down. You’re building your own personal arousal roadmap.
  • 🪷 Explore sensuality over sexuality: warm baths, soft fabrics, comforting smells — even if arousal doesn’t follow, it builds relationship with your body.
  • 💬 Be radically honest with partners: say when you’re not in the mood, or when you're still finding your rhythm — vulnerability builds trust, not distance.
🔍 What do SIS and SES actually mean?

SIS (Sexual Inhibition System) is your internal brake — it slows down arousal in response to emotional disconnection, stress, or fear. A medium SIS means you’re aware of those signals, but they don’t completely take over.

SES (Sexual Excitation System) is your gas pedal — it responds to arousal cues like emotional intimacy or physical touch. A low SES means your system needs stronger or more specific conditions to feel desire — and that’s completely valid.

Your system isn’t passive — it’s just discerning. You don’t rush toward arousal. You move when it feels right — and that kind of wisdom is rare.
