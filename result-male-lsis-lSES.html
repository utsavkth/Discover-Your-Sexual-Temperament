Your Sexual Temperament Summary

Profile: Low Inhibition (SIS) + Low Excitation (SES)

Hey friend, your sexual temperament is grounded and steady. You don’t feel blocked or anxious about sex (low brakes), but you also don’t experience intense or frequent arousal unless something truly clicks.

💡 What This Looks Like in Real Life

You’re probably not driven by constant sexual thoughts. You might enjoy sex and intimacy, but you don’t chase it. And when it does happen, it may take a while for you to get fully engaged. That doesn’t mean you’re cold — it means your system just doesn’t rush.

You may have been told you should want sex more, or feel confused when others seem to be running hot while you’re running neutral. But this isn’t a flaw. It’s just your baseline. You don’t need to be “fired up” all the time to be a deeply sexual person.

🧠 For Men Like You

Your profile is actually pretty chill — low anxiety, low pressure, low overdrive. But that ease can also lead to misunderstandings. Partners might think you’re not interested. You might wonder, “Why don’t I feel more?” But there’s nothing missing. You just need meaningful cues to really feel activated.

Your sexual expression is likely tied more to connection, comfort, and timing than urgency or raw desire. And when those conditions are present? You show up — fully, calmly, and genuinely.

✅ Tips That Work for Your Natural Tempo

  • 🌱 Don’t force the spark: your system doesn’t respond well to pressure or performance. Let arousal come to you in its own time.
  • 💬 Communicate your pace: let partners know that you’re open and interested, even if your desire unfolds slowly.
  • 🧘 Explore slow sensuality: massage, long kisses, shared baths — you may feel more tuned in through body presence than mental arousal.
  • 🎧 Use subtle stimulation: music, voice, emotional storytelling — these can awaken your SES in softer ways than visual stimulation.
  • 💡 Redefine desire: it’s not about hunger. It’s about curiosity, safety, and depth. And you’ve got all of that.
🔍 What do SIS and SES actually mean?

SIS (Sexual Inhibition System) is your brake — it slows arousal in response to stress, fear, or emotional disconnection. A low SIS means you’re generally relaxed and unbothered by pressure or shame.

SES (Sexual Excitation System) is your gas pedal — it responds to stimulation, emotional cues, or erotic energy. A low SES means you need specific or strong signals to feel turned on, and it may take time to build.

This combo doesn’t mean you’re not sexual. It means your sexuality is more quiet and selective — and that’s a beautiful thing.
