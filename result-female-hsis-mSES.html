Your Sexual Temperament Summary

Profile: High Inhibition (SIS) + Medium Excitation (SES)

Hey friend, based on your responses, your sexual temperament reflects:

  • 🚦 High sensitivity to internal and external "brakes" (SIS)
  • 💡 A balanced response to sexual "go-signals" (SES)

💭 What This Means

You’re someone whose desire doesn’t arrive on command — and that’s completely okay. For you, arousal needs to feel emotionally anchored. Your mind scans for safety before your body can say yes. Stress, judgment, awkwardness, or rushing? Total shutdown.

But here's the beauty: when the brakes are low and the vibe is right — emotional closeness, warmth, playful connection — you can ease into arousal with presence and grace. You're not wired for friction. You’re wired for flow.

👩‍🦰 A Note for Women Like You

If you’ve ever thought, “Why can’t I just want it more?” — please know, you’re not alone. Most messaging around sex assumes that desire is instant, always-on, and mostly physical. But your body works differently. You need trust, safety, and time — not a performance.

This isn’t about low libido. It’s about high intelligence in your nervous system. You feel deeply, so you need space to arrive fully.

✅ Try These (Not Just “Get in the Mood”)

  • 💆‍♀️ Build trust first: emotional security is your greatest aphrodisiac. You don’t “ease into sex” — you ease into safety.
  • 🌿 Minimize distractions: dim lights, soft clothes, silent phones — this clears the emotional noise your brake picks up.
  • 💬 Voice your boundaries: say when you need to slow down, pause, or shift — this isn’t rejection; it’s co-regulation.
  • 🪞 Reflect on context: keep track of moments where arousal felt easy — what was present? Who were you with? What wasn’t there?
  • 🌀 De-shame your pace: “slow to start” doesn’t mean “less sexual.” It means more depth, more richness, more realness.
🔍 What do SIS and SES actually mean?

SIS (Sexual Inhibition System) is your internal brake. It notices any signal that something’s off — emotionally, physically, or mentally — and it slows your arousal. A high SIS means this system is highly responsive to stress, distraction, or fear.

SES (Sexual Excitation System) is your accelerator. It picks up on erotic cues — like skin contact, sensual vibes, or emotional closeness — and turns the ignition. Medium SES means you respond well, but not instantly or automatically. You need the right vibe.

This profile isn't a dysfunction. It’s an invitation to be more attuned, intentional, and compassionate with your body’s rhythms.
