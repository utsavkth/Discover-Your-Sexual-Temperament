Your Sexual Temperament Summary

Profile: Medium Inhibition (SIS) + High Excitation (SES)

Hey friend, your results show a powerful combo: you're highly responsive to sexual and emotional cues (SES), while still carrying a healthy level of internal caution (SIS).

💡 What This Looks Like in Real Life

You might often find yourself feeling aroused by connection, energy, and playful flirtation. A meaningful glance, a kind gesture, or a cozy evening can all become ignition points. Your SES is sensitive — it notices, absorbs, and responds.

At the same time, your SIS quietly monitors for disconnection, stress, or emotional mismatch. If something feels off, you may suddenly pause, check out, or go cold — not because you aren’t interested, but because your body is asking, “Is this still safe?” It’s not a bug. It’s built-in wisdom.

👩‍🦰 For Women Like You

Society often assumes that if a woman is easily aroused, she must be "always ready." But your profile says otherwise — you may feel desire often, but it still needs to be held gently. Your flame burns hot, but it needs the right conditions to stay lit.

When you're with someone who gets that — someone who listens, checks in, and creates space — you can thrive sexually. But when you're pressured or dismissed, your SIS kicks in to protect you. And that’s a superpower, not a problem.

✅ Practices That Support Your Fire

  • 🕯️ Curate your vibe: lighting, tone of voice, scent, warmth — these small signals turn your SES on in meaningful ways.
  • 🌬️ Manage emotional noise: small conflicts, stress, or internal doubts can subtly activate your SIS — clear them out.
  • 💬 Let desire breathe: talk openly about what builds your arousal — and what quietly kills it. That transparency protects your energy.
  • 🎶 Use arousal cues intentionally: music, memory, imagination — these are powerful tools for you. Don’t wait for desire; create space for it.
  • 🫶 Balance energy and empathy: your SES may go full throttle, but always listen to your SIS if it says pause. That’s emotional intelligence at work.
🔍 What do SIS and SES actually mean?

SIS (Sexual Inhibition System) is your brake — it pays attention to stress, anxiety, and emotional disconnect. Medium SIS means it doesn’t overreact, but it still taps the brakes if something doesn’t feel quite right.

SES (Sexual Excitation System) is your gas pedal. Yours is high, which means you respond quickly and deeply to sexual and sensual cues when things are emotionally and physically aligned.

This combo makes you passionate, playful, and aware — a truly empowered sexual style.
