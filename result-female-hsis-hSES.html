Your Sexual Temperament Summary

Profile: High Inhibition (SIS) + High Excitation (SES)

Hey friend, your system says YES and NO at the same time — and that’s not a contradiction. It’s a form of brilliance.

You scored high on both the gas pedal (SES) and the brake (SIS). That means you can experience powerful desire — but only when your internal and external world says it’s safe. You’re someone who feels deeply, but also protects deeply.

💡 What This Looks Like in Real Life

Maybe you’ve had nights where a single touch or glance sent your mind racing with desire — your body all in. But another night, a tiny stressor — a messy room, a moment of self-doubt, a partner rushing things — and suddenly it’s like someone yanked the handbrake. It’s not random. It’s how your system was built.

This doesn’t mean you’re “too complicated” or “not in the mood enough.” It means you need the right context — and when you have that, your desire can be rich, deep, and sustaining.

👩‍🦰 For Women Like You

If you grew up with mixed messages about sex — like "be sexy but not too much," or "want it but don’t show it" — your brakes might be hypersensitive from years of pressure or fear of judgment. And if no one told you how your unique wiring works, you might’ve assumed you were broken.

You’re not. You just need space that honors both your desire and your boundaries. That honors you.

✅ Try These Instead of “Just Relax”

  • 🧠 Mentally rehearse arousal: fantasize, journal, or visualize erotic scenes where you feel fully safe and desired — this helps prep your SES.
  • 🎧 Engage your senses: scent, sound, lighting — sensuality starts in the nervous system, not just the genitals.
  • 💬 Name your brakes without guilt: "I want this, but I need to slow down" is sexy honesty, not rejection.
  • 🛁 Design your environment: tidy up, use warm lighting, put on your playlist — let your space say “you’re safe to open.”
  • 💗 Be curious, not critical: when desire disappears, don’t shame it. Ask it what it needs.
🔍 What do SIS and SES actually mean?

SIS (Sexual Inhibition System) is your brake. It responds to stress, worry, discomfort, or anything that signals “not safe right now.” A high SIS means your brake is extra sensitive — even subtle stuff can make it kick in.

SES (Sexual Excitation System) is your accelerator. It notices erotic cues, closeness, and sensual details. A high SES means you can get turned on easily — but only if your brakes aren’t in the way.

Both systems are normal. The magic comes from understanding how yours dance together.
