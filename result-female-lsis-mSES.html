Your Sexual Temperament Summary

Profile: Low Inhibition (SIS) + Medium Excitation (SES)

Hey friend, your profile shows a low tendency to hit the brakes (SIS) — and a moderate, steady response to sexual cues (SES). That’s a beautiful balance of openness and patience.

💡 What This Looks Like in Real Life

You likely don’t get overwhelmed by stress, shame, or anxiety when it comes to intimacy. Your mind is open to eroticism, and you’re not easily thrown off course. That’s your low SIS doing its job — it’s not guarding the gate too tightly.

But you also don’t get turned on super fast. You’re not necessarily the “can’t-wait-to-tear-your-clothes-off” type — and that’s fine. Your SES (sexual accelerator) responds when the vibe is right, not just when the opportunity shows up. You might feel desire slowly build over time or in emotionally rich contexts.

👩‍🦰 For Women Like You

You might feel “in between” — not easily blocked, but not easily ignited either. That in-between zone can feel confusing if you're surrounded by stories of passion that’s either constant or non-existent. But you’re not lacking. You’re layered.

Your sexuality may thrive in subtlety: in soft moments, meaningful touches, quiet intimacy. You don’t need to “turn it up” — you need to tune in.

✅ Ways to Deepen Your Experience

  • 🌿 Let intimacy flow in gradually: you don’t need a “spark” to begin — you need space to feel into the moment.
  • 🕯️ Use rituals to build connection: lighting a candle, soft music, or a shared bath can set the stage for your SES to respond.
  • 📖 Notice what subtly arouses you: emotional warmth, storytelling, gentle touch — all valid arousal cues for you.
  • 🔄 Don’t overthink consistency: some days, arousal will show up easily. Other days, it won’t. That’s not failure. That’s fluctuation.
  • 💌 Give yourself credit: not being “highly sexual” doesn't mean you’re disconnected. Your body knows when it’s ready — trust that.
🔍 What do SIS and SES actually mean?

SIS (Sexual Inhibition System) is your brake. It slows down arousal in response to fear, shame, distraction, or discomfort. A low SIS means you’re not overly inhibited — your body is generally willing and open.

SES (Sexual Excitation System) is your gas pedal. It revs things up when you're emotionally engaged or erotically stimulated. A medium SES means you can get there — but it’s all about timing, context, and connection.

Your combo invites exploration without pressure — a “slow yes” that’s still deeply valid.
