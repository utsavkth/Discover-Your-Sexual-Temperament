Sexy Contexts Worksheet

🧠 What turns you on — and what shuts you down — is about more than “sex drive”

This isn’t a worksheet to submit. It’s a mirror. These reflections are here to help you understand how your unique sexual wiring responds to environment, emotion, safety, and story.

Use these prompts in your journal, a voice note, or just while daydreaming. You don’t need to write. You just need to listen to your body — with compassion.

💔 What Hits the Brakes?

Think of your Sexual Inhibition System (SIS) as the brake pedal. It activates when something feels off — even subtly.

  • Performance Pressure: “I feel like I have to act sexy or I’ll ruin the moment.”
  • Fear of Rejection: “If I ask for this, will they pull away?”
  • Environment: Bright lights. Unwashed sheets. Noise outside. Kids in the next room.
  • Body Image: “I don’t feel desirable today. I just feel bloated and exhausted.”

Reflection: What are 3 situations or thoughts that instantly shut you down?

🔥 What Hits the Accelerator?

Your SES (Sexual Excitation System) is the gas pedal. It responds to arousing cues — but not always the ones you expect.

  • Emotional Intimacy: “They held eye contact and I melted.”
  • Anticipation: A flirtatious message during the day builds excitement.
  • Sensory Cues: Soft touch. Familiar scent. Warm breath near your neck.
  • Reassurance: “You’re safe. You’re not too much. I want you here.”

Reflection: Recall a moment when desire felt effortless. What helped that happen?

🎯 What Are Your Ideal Contexts?

Desire often blooms in the right context — not out of nowhere.

  • After connection: A deep conversation. Emotional repair. Laughter in bed.
  • Slow mornings: No alarms. No expectations. Just lingering touches and soft sheets.
  • After self-care: When you’ve had time to shower, stretch, or breathe.

Reflection: If you could create your dream context for arousal, what would it include?

📝 Prompts for Reflection

  • “I feel safe and open when...”
  • “One thing I secretly want but haven’t voiced is...”
  • “If I could remove one internal barrier, it would be...”

💡 Myth vs Reality

Myth: Desire should be spontaneous.
Reality: Most people experience responsive desire. It comes after closeness begins.

Myth: Low libido means you're broken.
Reality: Often, it just means your brakes are activated more easily.

Myth: Men want more, women want less.
Reality: Gender has nothing to do with it. Context does.

🚗 Now That You Know...

Once you’ve discovered your temperament (SIS/SES), this page gives you language. Start where it feels safe. Explore slowly. Share with someone you trust — or just keep it for you.

Small changes in context can unlock deep shifts in desire.
