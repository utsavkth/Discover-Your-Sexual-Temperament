Your Sexual Temperament Summary

Profile: Low Inhibition (SIS) + Low Excitation (SES)

Hey friend, your results suggest a gentle and steady sexual temperament — low brakes and low acceleration. You’re not easily shut down, but you also don’t feel a strong drive to pursue sexual experiences spontaneously. And that’s perfectly valid.

💡 What This Looks Like in Real Life

You probably don’t get aroused by random cues or fiery chemistry. Sex might not be something you actively crave unless conditions are just right. You can be open to intimacy — but it often unfolds from closeness, warmth, and trust over time, rather than sudden sparks.

This can sometimes feel confusing in a culture that glorifies urgency, passion, and “can’t-keep-my-hands-off-you” vibes. But your pace isn’t broken — it’s real, grounded, and reflective of a quieter nervous system.

👩‍🦰 For Women Like You

You might have asked yourself: “Why don’t I feel that hunger everyone talks about?” or “Am I missing something?” The truth is, you're not. Your system is just more subtle in how it processes desire. And that’s not a flaw — it’s a different kind of strength.

You may thrive in long-term emotional intimacy, where sex becomes part of connection — not a separate, fiery thing. And when that connection is strong? Your arousal can surprise even you.

✅ Supportive Approaches for Your Temperament

  • 🕯️ Start with emotional closeness: long hugs, eye contact, quiet time together — these lay the foundation for deeper arousal.
  • 🪞 Give yourself permission to not be “in the mood” all the time: this doesn’t make you any less loving or intimate.
  • 📚 Explore arousal gently: reading, daydreaming, fantasy — all can be helpful without pressure to perform.
  • 🎨 Be creative with intimacy: sensual massage, shared rituals, playful exploration — let intimacy be more than just sex.
  • 💗 Focus on “what brings me closer?” rather than “how do I get turned on?” Your answer is often found in connection, not in intensity.
🔍 What do SIS and SES actually mean?

SIS (Sexual Inhibition System) is your brain’s brake. It slows arousal when something feels off — stress, fear, or distraction. A low SIS means you’re generally not held back by those things. You’re open.

SES (Sexual Excitation System) is your gas pedal. It responds to erotic cues and revs up arousal. A low SES means those signals don’t trigger you easily — you may need more time or intimacy to get there.

This doesn’t mean you’re not sexual. It means your sexuality is quiet, gentle, and often tied to deeper emotional context.
