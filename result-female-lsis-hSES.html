Your Sexual Temperament Summary

Profile: Low Inhibition (SIS) + High Excitation (SES)

Hey friend, your results show a low sensitivity to brakes (SIS) and a high sensitivity to sexual cues (SES). In plain terms? You're someone who’s highly responsive, curious, and open — when the vibe hits right.

💡 What This Looks Like in Real Life

You may find that desire often shows up quickly, even unexpectedly. A smell, a touch, a memory, a scene — and suddenly your body is ready. You don’t need long mental preparation or complex emotional conditions. You’re wired to respond.

But that doesn’t mean you’re “always in the mood” — or that you should be. Even with low brakes, your nervous system still needs respect, consent, and care. Desire may be quick to light, but it still deserves depth.

👩‍🦰 For Women Like You

In many ways, your profile aligns with cultural expectations of how women “should” feel about sex — spontaneous, turned on, enthusiastic. But sometimes, that creates pressure. You might’ve felt guilty when desire didn’t show up, or wondered why partners expect you to always be responsive just because you often are.

Here’s the truth: your profile is a gift, but it still requires boundaries, self-checks, and rest. You are more than your sexual readiness.

✅ Suggestions That Support Your Balance

  • 🔥 Honor your responsiveness: if something turns you on quickly, don’t question it — follow it with curiosity.
  • 🛑 Practice pause and check-in: even if brakes are light, ask yourself “Do I want this? Am I emotionally okay?”
  • 🪷 Keep sex nourishing, not just exciting: high SES doesn’t mean you always need novelty. Comfort and safety matter too.
  • 💬 Communicate expectations: tell partners, “Yes, I get turned on fast — but that doesn’t mean I always want to act on it.”
  • 📌 Set boundaries for sustainability: your gas pedal is strong — but even sports cars need brakes.
🔍 What do SIS and SES actually mean?

SIS (Sexual Inhibition System) is your internal brake — it slows you down in response to stress, fear, shame, or discomfort. A low SIS means you’re not easily disrupted by distractions or worries.

SES (Sexual Excitation System) is your accelerator — it revs you up when your body senses something sexy, intimate, or emotionally engaging. A high SES means your system is tuned in and ready when the conditions are right.

Together, this combo means fast ignition — but fast isn’t always “better.” It’s about knowing yourself, not performing for others.
