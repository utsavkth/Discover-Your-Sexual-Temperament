Your Sexual Temperament Summary

Profile: Low Inhibition (SIS) + Medium Excitation (SES)

Hey friend, your sexual system moves like water — calm, open, and responsive. You’re not easily blocked or thrown off (low brakes), but you also don’t rush into arousal unless the emotional and sensory conditions feel right.

💡 What This Looks Like in Real Life

You’re not constantly seeking sex, but you’re open to it — especially when there’s connection, comfort, and warmth. A shared joke, a long hug, a soft voice — these can quietly spark your desire. It builds slowly, but steadily, and you rarely feel the internal pressure to “be ready.”

Your body is relaxed about sex. You don’t shut down easily, and you don’t overreact. That makes you emotionally steady and deeply attuned — especially in relationships where intimacy feels like a shared rhythm, not a performance.

🌈 For Those Who Walk Their Own Path

Whether you’re genderfluid, agender, non-binary, or simply uninterested in strict sexual norms — your profile reflects autonomy. You engage when it feels right. You express yourself without force. You deserve partners who value that softness, not rush it.

This kind of arousal isn’t about intensity. It’s about resonance. And that’s a deeply valid — and often under-celebrated — way of being.

✅ Ways to Support Your Natural Rhythm

  • 🌙 Build slow, sensual environments: dim lights, soft music, no pressure — let your system ease into presence.
  • 💬 Use emotional cues to spark arousal: conversation, shared laughter, quiet support — these fuel your SES better than raw intensity.
  • 🛏️ Welcome intimacy in many forms: touch, closeness, shared space — even if it doesn’t lead to sex, it builds the bridge.
  • 📖 Reflect on your turn-on patterns: what felt safe? What surprised you? Your body is always teaching you if you’re listening.
  • 💡 Let slowness be powerful: your pace isn’t something to fix — it’s something to protect. You don’t rush into connection — you arrive.
🔍 What do SIS and SES actually mean?

SIS (Sexual Inhibition System) is your brake — it slows you down in response to fear, shame, or pressure. A low SIS means you’re generally open, emotionally relaxed, and not easily thrown off course.

SES (Sexual Excitation System) is your gas pedal — it revs you up when things feel emotionally and sensually right. A medium SES means your arousal is available — but not automatic. It needs a real vibe.

This profile is grounded, calm, and quietly confident. You know what feels right — and you don’t force what doesn’t.
