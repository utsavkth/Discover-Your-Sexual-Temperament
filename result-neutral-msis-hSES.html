Your Sexual Temperament Summary

Profile: Medium Inhibition (SIS) + High Excitation (SES)

Hey friend, your system is vibrant and alert — desire shows up easily when the vibe is right, but it won’t stick around if something feels off. You’re sensitive in both directions: you open fast, but you still care deeply about how safe, mutual, and emotionally aligned the moment feels.

💡 What This Looks Like in Real Life

You might get turned on from a look, a touch, a word — your arousal system is quick and curious. But even with all that responsiveness, you still have a filter: your emotional radar is active. If something doesn’t feel right — even subtly — you’ll pause or pull back.

This profile often confuses others. They see your openness and assume you’re “always ready.” But readiness doesn’t mean availability. You want sex to be grounded, reciprocal, and emotionally safe — not rushed, assumed, or one-sided.

🌈 For Fluid and Non-Binary Identities

You might not see yourself represented in most conversations about desire. But your pattern is real: emotionally attuned, sensually present, and fiercely self-respecting. You’re someone who blends sensitivity with strength. You don’t need to explain that duality — just honor it.

Your arousal is not a performance. It’s a response to real energy, real care, and real context. And that’s powerful.

✅ Tools to Balance Your Desire with Depth

  • 🔥 Use sensory cues that excite you: scent, sound, lighting, tone — these awaken your SES beautifully.
  • 💬 Talk openly about pacing: even with strong desire, you still get to pause and check in — especially if your SIS starts to whisper.
  • 🧠 Reflect when arousal drops: what shifted? What felt off? What would help you feel safe again?
  • 🌕 Use fantasy and creativity: your high SES responds well to stories, imagination, and safe exploration.
  • 🪞 Let desire be mutual, not expected: you deserve partners who meet you where you are — not just where they assume you’ll go.
🔍 What do SIS and SES actually mean?

SIS (Sexual Inhibition System) is your internal brake — it slows things down when something feels emotionally, mentally, or physically “off.” A medium SIS means you’re aware and responsive — but not hyper-reactive.

SES (Sexual Excitation System) is your gas pedal — it lights up when the vibe is right. A high SES means your body and mind are deeply sensitive to arousal cues — emotional, sensual, or imaginative.

This combo is powerful: it means you feel desire easily, but you still hold the steering wheel. Your body may say yes, but your heart gets the final word — and that’s wisdom.
