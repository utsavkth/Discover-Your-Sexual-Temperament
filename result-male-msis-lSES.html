Your Sexual Temperament Summary

Profile: Medium Inhibition (SIS) + Low Excitation (SES)

Hey friend, your sexual temperament reflects a thoughtful pace. You’re not easily shut down (moderate brakes), but you also don’t feel arousal rise quickly or automatically (low gas). That’s not a problem — it’s your body asking for safety, space, and connection before desire kicks in.

💡 What This Looks Like in Real Life

You might not think about sex as often as others seem to. Even when things are going well emotionally, it can take a while for you to feel sexually engaged. You’re open, but you’re also tuned in — you won’t just flip a switch unless it feels right.

At the same time, stress, awkward moments, or emotional distance can quietly slow things down or make you zone out. Your SIS is moderate — not hyper-vigilant, but definitely active. That means emotional alignment matters more than pressure or performance.

🧠 For Men Like You

Society rarely makes space for slower sexual rhythms in men. If you’ve ever wondered, “Why don’t I feel more?” — it’s not because anything’s wrong. You’re not broken. You’re just not easily flipped into arousal mode without the *right ingredients*.

When connection, comfort, and emotional tone line up, your desire can show up in a grounded, meaningful way. And that kind of desire? It’s deeply present, not just reactive.

✅ What Helps You Feel More Alive, Not Just “Aroused”

  • 🧘 Start with calm, not stimulation: your system responds best to low-pressure, emotionally rich moments — not intensity.
  • 💬 Talk about emotional readiness: arousal for you often starts in the heart and mind — naming that helps your partner meet you there.
  • 🌙 Create quiet space for desire: a long walk, slow touch, or just undistracted presence — these are powerful mood-setters for your SES.
  • 🪞 Notice subtle cues: what made you feel most connected in past intimate moments? What killed the vibe? Those patterns matter.
  • 📓 Journal your inner landscape: this can help you decode what lights your SES up — and what shuts it down.
🔍 What do SIS and SES actually mean?

SIS (Sexual Inhibition System) is your brake — it activates in response to pressure, emotional disconnect, or mental stress. A medium SIS means you can flow in the right environment, but your system still notices if something feels off.

SES (Sexual Excitation System) is your accelerator — it responds to turn-ons, emotional cues, and erotic stimuli. A low SES means those cues need to be stronger or more specific for arousal to happen.

Bottom line: you’re emotionally attuned. You don’t rush. You arrive fully, when it matters — and that’s your power.
